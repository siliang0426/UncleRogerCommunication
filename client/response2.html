A patient have been experiencing frequent heartburn lately. What could be causing it, and how can I relieve the discomfort?

Olivia Reynolds

There are several potential causes, including diet, lifestyle, and underlying medical conditions like gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD). To start, try avoiding trigger foods like spicy or acidic items, eating smaller meals, and not lying down right after eating. If these changes don't help, we can discuss further evaluation and potential medications.

Dr. Sarah Mitchell

Heartburn can be uncomfortable, and it's important to determine the cause. It may be related to patient's diet or certain behaviors like eating late at night. You can explore patient's dietary habits, and I may recommend an over-the-counter antacid to relieve symptoms temporarily. If the problem persists, we can discuss more targeted treatments.
